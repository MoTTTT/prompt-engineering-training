# Module 6 — Enterprise Governance and Compliance

**Audience**: Technical leads, architects, team leads, management — and any developer who wants to understand the organisational context for AI adoption\
**Prerequisites**: None required. Module 1 provides useful context but is not a dependency.\
**Duration**: ~3 hours

---

## Why This Module Exists

The technical challenge of building AI features is the smaller half of the problem. The larger half is organisational: who decides what AI can be used for, how you comply with emerging regulation, how you make the business case, and how you stop a good idea from becoming a liability.

Most organisations adopting AI have either no governance in place, or governance that is being made up reactively as problems surface. This module gives you the frameworks, language, and practical tools to do it proactively.

---

## What You Will Learn

1. **Governance Frameworks** — NIST AI RMF and ISO 42001 in plain language; what they require in practice
2. **Model Selection and Vendor Risk** — how to evaluate LLM providers as business risks, not just technical choices
3. **Regulatory Landscape** — EU AI Act, GDPR, sector-specific considerations; what you must do, not just what you should
4. **LLMOps** — running AI systems in production; prompt drift, model versioning, monitoring, incident response
5. **Change Management and ROI** — getting organisational buy-in; measuring value; making the business case

## Key Themes

- **Governance is not a constraint on AI adoption — it is what makes AI adoption sustainable.** Teams that skip governance move fast early and pay later.
- **The regulatory landscape is moving.** What you do today needs to be auditable and adjustable, not frozen.
- **The business case for AI needs numbers.** "It feels faster" is not enough. This module gives you the measurement framework.
