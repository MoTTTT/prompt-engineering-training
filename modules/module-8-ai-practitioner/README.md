# Module 8 — AI for Office Professionals

**Audience**: Non-technical staff who use Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint as primary work tools. No programming knowledge required.
**Duration**: ~2.5 hours
**Prerequisites**: None

---

## Overview

AI tools are now embedded in the applications that office professionals use every day. Microsoft 365 Copilot is integrated into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. Browser-based AI assistants from multiple providers are available to anyone with an internet connection. The question for most office workers is no longer whether AI tools are available — it is how to use them effectively, safely, and in accordance with your organisation's rules.

This module addresses all three. It is written for people who have no interest in building AI systems and no need to understand how they work technically. What you need is a practical mental model, a set of safe habits, and a clear understanding of what your organisation permits.

No coding is required at any point in this module.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

- Describe what Microsoft 365 Copilot does and how it integrates with Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams
- Identify which AI tools your organisation has approved and understand the difference between approved and unapproved tools
- Apply the four-pillar ICIO structure to write effective prompts for your own work tasks
- Describe what data must never be entered into an AI tool, and apply a pre-prompt checklist to protect client and organisational data
- Explain in plain terms what GDPR and POPIA require of you as an office professional using AI tools
- Know what to do if you believe an AI tool has been misused or if you have accidentally sent confidential data to a cloud service

## Prerequisites

None. This module assumes no technical background. If you are familiar with how to use Word, Excel, and PowerPoint, you have everything you need to begin.

---

## A Note on Coding

This module contains no code. There are no programming exercises, no command-line instructions, and no configuration files. All examples are in plain English and use tools that work through a normal graphical interface.

If you are a developer or technical staff member, this module may still be useful as a reference for communicating AI safe-use principles to non-technical colleagues. However, the primary audience is office professionals who manage documents, data, and communications as their core work.

---

## How to Use This Module

Work through the sections in order — each section builds on the previous one. Section 01 establishes what the tools are. Section 02 shows what you can do with them. Section 03 establishes the safety habits you need before using them with real work content. Section 04 addresses your obligations under your organisation's policies and applicable law.

The check-your-understanding questions at the end of each section are designed for reflection, not assessment. There are no wrong answers in the sense of failing — they are prompts to think about how the content applies to your specific role and organisation.
