# Trainee Profile Template

**Instructions for coordinator:** Give this structure to the trainee before their first session.
Ask them to paste it as their opening prompt, replacing the bracketed examples with their own answers.
Alex will extract and record the responses as the trainee profile.

---

```
1. Who I am
1.1 Name — [e.g. Norma]
1.2 Profession — [e.g. Industrial Psychologist]
1.3 How long I have been in this profession — [e.g. 20+ years]

2. The role I am currently playing
2.1 [One sentence describing your primary function — e.g. I write medico-legal reports for injured clients to assess the impact of their injuries on their careers and earnings]
2.2 [Describe your end-to-end workflow, step by step — e.g. I am instructed by attorneys, then interview clients, then do research on their circumstances, then write a report to the attorneys, then handle follow-up questions, then do pretrial consultations, then testify in court if needed, then bill when matters are settled]

3. Current challenges I have
3.1 [First challenge — be specific about the pain point]
3.2 [Second challenge]
3.3 [Further challenges as needed]

4. What I want to get out of this course
4.1 [Your primary goal — e.g. I want to optimise my workflow using AI and solve specific problems]
4.2 [Any constraints — e.g. I do not need comprehensive prompt engineering knowledge, I need practical application]
4.3 [Any specific outputs — e.g. I need a session summary document at the end of each session]
```

---

## Notes for coordinators

- The trainee should submit this as their **first prompt** of the first session.
- Alex will use this context throughout the programme — all examples will be anchored to the trainee's domain.
- Section 4 is particularly important: it lets Alex prioritise modules and skip irrelevant content.
- If the trainee is also acting as a reviewer (e.g. reviewing on behalf of the training coordinator), they should note that in section 4.
